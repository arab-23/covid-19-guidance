Comment fonctionne la subvention

Eligible employers who had any drop in revenue can now qualify for the wage subsidy, starting on the claim periods that began July 5.

July 5 to December 19, 2020

For claims periods starting July 5, 2020:

  • the subsidy rate is now variable, depending on your revenue drop
  • you no longer need at least a 30% revenue drop to qualify, which means you can keep getting the subsidy as employees return to work and your income improves
  • employers who had a drop in revenue of at least 50% over a period of three months can get an extra top-up amount
  • some employees on leave who were not eligible before may be eligible now
  • you can use the current month’s revenue drop or the previous month’s, whichever works in your favour
  • for periods 5 and 6, you can use the old rules and calculation if it works in your favour (the “safe harbour” option)
  • the rate of subsidy will decline beginning in period 7, as business returns to normal

Download infographic: Take another look at the CEWS

March 15 to July 4, 2020

For claims periods between March 15 to July 4, 2020:

  • Employers are subject to a minimum of 15% (period 1) to 30% (periods 2 to 4) revenue drop
  • Once the revenue drop is met, they qualify for the subsidy 75% of the eligible employees remuneration, up to a maximum of $847/week.

How you apply

  1. Determine if you are an eligible employer
  2. Determine which employees you can claim
  3. Use the calculator to find out how much subsidy you can claim under the new rules. There are two calculator options:
    • a new web form
    • a downloadable spreadsheet
  4. Sign-in to your CRA account to input the values of your claim

Your base rate is a standard 60%

This means:

  • Eligible employees earning $1,129/week or more would get $1,129 x 60% = $677/week
  • Eligible employees earning less than $1,129/week would get 60% of their remuneration, up to $677/week.

Your base rate 1.2 times your revenue drop %

This means:

  • If you had 20% revenue drop, your base rate is 20% x 1.2 = 24%
  • Eligible employees earning $1,129/week or more would get $1,129 x 24% = $271/week
  • Eligible employees earning less than $1,129/week would get 24% of their remuneration, up to $271/week.

Note:

  • Claim periods 5 and 6 have a maximum base rate of 60%
  • Claim periods 7 to 9 maximum base rates will be reduced from 60% to 20%
