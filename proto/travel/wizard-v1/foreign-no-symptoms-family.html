Who can come to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Family

You indicated that:

  • you are a foreign national
  • you are not showing symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • you are an immediate family member of a Canadian citizen or a landed permanent resident

Based on your answers, you may be able to come to Canada. Final decisions are taken at the border, by the border officer.

As an immediate family member of a Canadian citizen or a landed permanent resident, you may be allowed in Canada if you will be staying for at least 15 days. If you’re staying for less than 15 days, you may be able to come to Canada for a different reason, see other reasons for entry into Canada.

An immediate family is defined as a:

  • spouse or common-law partner
  • dependent child
  • dependent child of a dependent child
  • parent or step-parent
  • guardian or tutor

For more information, go to Reuniting with qualifying immediate family.

You may have to follow certain provincial or territorial requirements, depending on your destination.
