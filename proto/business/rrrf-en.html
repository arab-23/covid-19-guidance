Regional Relief and Recovery Fund: COVID-19

From: Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada

The COVID-19 pandemic has already had significant impacts on businesses, workers and communities across the country.

The Government of Canada recognizes this and will be there to support you during and after the crisis.

Support for affected businesses and communities

The Regional Relief and Recovery Fund (RRRF) will help

  • mitigate the financial pressure experienced by businesses and organizations - allowing you to continue operations, including paying your employees
  • support projects by businesses, organizations and communities to prepare for a successful recovery

The nearly $1 billion in support is divided into two initiatives

  • $675 million to support regional economies, businesses, organizations and communities in regions all across Canada;
  • $287 million to support the national network of Community Futures Development Corporations, which will specifically target small businesses and rural communities across the country.

How to apply

Apply to the RRRF through your Regional Development Agency

  • Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency (ACOA)
  • Canada Economic Development for Quebec Regions (CEDQ)
  • Federal Economic Development Agency for Southern Ontario (FedDev)
  • Federal Economic Development Initiative in Northern Ontario (FedNor)
  • Western Economic Diversification Canada (WD)
  • Canadian Northern Economic Development Agency (CanNor)

Other measures to help you

  • Deferred payments on current contribution agreements with your regional development agency for three months
  • CanNor's Northern Business Relief Fund (NBRF) provides short-term support for ongoing operational costs to small- and medium-sized territorial businesses
  • Canadian Seafood Stabilization Fund provides temporary funding for fish and seafood processing businesses
